Alternative RNA splicing results in multiple transcripts of the same gene, possibly coding different protein isoforms that may differ in their protein domains. Although transcripts and domains information can be extracted from public databases, such a process requires the integration of data from several sources and it is tedious and unfeasible for genome-wide analysis. DoChaP integrates transcripts and domains information to facilitate the study of alternative splicing effects on domain structure. Identifying such an effect can lead to testable hypotheses in the form of 'protein X loses domain Y in state Z'.  

DoChaP integrates information on transcripts and protein domains data from the NCBI and UCSC databases into a single database.

Sources:

  • NCBI - RefSeq database (.gpff GeneBank files) and the gene2ensemble table from Gene database
  • UCSC - Tables (ncbiRefSeq, kgXref and knownGene) of the latest genome versions for each species.
