How to use DoChaP

On the homepage of DoChaP, you will find a search window where you can insert your search term and choose a species.

A - Species:

Select a species. Five species are currently available: Human (hg38), Mouse (mm39), Rat (rn6), Zebrafish (danRer11), and Frog (xenTro9).

We invite users to contact us to request adding a specific species.

B - Search by gene, transcript or protein:

In the search box, type your gene of interest using one of the following identifiers: gene symbol, gene synonym, NCBI’s Gene ID, RefSeq transcript or protein ID (starts with NM_ /XM_/NP_/XP_), ENSEMBL gene, transcript or protein ID (starts with ENS).

Finally, click the “Search” button. Note, for genes with many transcripts the search might take longer.

How to interpret DoChaP results?

After DoChaP has found your gene of interest, it will present a page similar to Figure 2.


Panel A – Display options:

The display can be adjusted by using the “Display” dropdown to present:

  • All views (default) – Shows the genomic structure of the transcript (on the left) and the corresponding coding region of the transcript and the protein domain composition (on the right), for all transcripts
  • Transcript & Protein view – Shows the coding transcript and the protein domain composition (similar to the right side of the “All views” option), for all transcripts
  • Genomic view – Shows only the transcript structure for all transcripts
  • Transcript view – Shows only the coding regions for all transcripts
  • Protein view – Shows only the protein domain composition for all transcripts

“Hide predicted records”

  • Predicted records are transcripts that have not yet been validated by the providers of the source information. For example: transcripts or proteins with a RefSeq ID that starts with XM_ or XP_, respectively. By checking this check box, you will hide all predicted transcripts from your results.

“Save as PDF”

  • Use this option to save the results of your gene of interest to a high-quality PDF file.

Panel B – Displayed range adjustment

This panel enables you to control the genomic coordinates (left side) and the coding region coordinates (right side). The black slider represents the region displayed. The diamond-shaped location handles at the ends of the slider represent the start and end positions of the displayed region. The location handles can be moved along the slider by the user to control which part of the gene or transcript is shown. The buttons below the slider allow zooming in and out. Double click on an exon in the Genome view will zoom in to the relevant region in the transcript & protein view.

The loci of the gene of interest is shown above the slider in the Genome panel.


Panel C – Genomic and transcript coordinates

Left side: The black bar represents the axis of the genomic location, with tick marks above the bar indicating genomic coordinates. Below the bar is an arrow representing the coding strand (left arrow for reverse strand and right arrow for forward strand). Note, when a gene encoded on the reverse strand is shown, the genomic axis is flipped to allow the first exon to be on the left and the last exon to be on the right, in order for the direction to be consistent with the transcript and protein visualization.

Right side: The black bar represents the axis of the transcript and the protein length, starting at zero. Upper tick marks represent nucleotides, and lower tick marks represent amino acids. All transcripts and proteins in panels D-G are aligned to zero. Only the coding region of the transcripts is shown (no introns and no UTRs).


Panel D – Genomic visualization of transcripts:

The genome is represented by a thin black line, and exons are represented by colored boxes. UTRs are represented as thinner boxes at the edges of the transcript. Different colors represent different exons of the gene, and the colors are consistent across transcript and protein visualizations. Note that colors are set by the coding region, so that exons that are different in their UTRs but identical in their coding region will have the same color. Conversely, alternative exons that are different in even one base in the coding region will have different colors. The transcript ID is shown above each transcript, and clicking on it will show additional information on the transcript. The 'Hide transcript' option ( ) will hide the transcript from the visualization. Hovering over an exon will show the genomic position of the exon and its ordinal number in the transcript.


Panel E – Coding mRNA view:

This panel shows the coding region of the mature mRNA, without UTRs. All the exons (within the coding region) are joined; each color represents a different exon within the transcript and is consistent across all the transcripts of the same gene. The transcript ID is shown above the mRNA visualization, and clicking on it will show additional information. Hovering over the exons will show the ordinal number of the exon in the transcript and its length.


Panel F – Domain composition view:

This panel shows the protein isoform encoded from the genomic region of the transcript on the left and the corresponding coding region shown above. The thin black line represents the entire protein. Domains are shown as elliptical shapes and are colored according to the exons encoding them. A domain encoded from multiple exons will be colored in multiple colors according to the colors of the exons encoding it. Color changes within domains represent splice junctions. The protein ID is displayed above the domain visualization, and clicking on it will show additional information. Hovering over a domain will show its full name, position on the protein, length in amino acids, external ID and a link to the external source (Pfam, SMART, CDD, TIGRFAMs, and InterPro).

In the case of overlapping domains, a single domain with a double frame is displayed. Hovering will show the number of overlapping domains, and clicking will open a detailed visualization showing all the domains (Figure 3).

 

Panels D,E and F appear for each transcript of the gene. Different transcripts are separated by thin horizontal blue line.

 

Compare species tab:

The “Compare Species“ feature enables the user to visualize and compare orthologous genes from different species.

To compare transcripts, choose the first species you wish to compare and a gene of interest, then click “Find orthology“. DoChaP will look for all the orthologues of your gene in the other available species. Once completed, the “Species to compare“ drop-down box will become enabled. Select the species for which the comparison is required and the relevant gene from the given options. Orthologues are defined by Ensembl Compara. Genes that were not found in Ensembl Compara but share the same gene symbol in a different species are also available for viewing.

The results will present all the transcripts of the gene in the first species on the left and all the transcripts of the gene in the second species on the right, in the same format as that described above for the DoChaP main page.
